 New all-wheel-drive versions in 2008 (Carrera 4 and 4S). In 2011, Porsche launched a new Carrera GTS which had a upgraded 3.8-litre engine generating with 413 bhp. The looks barely changed, but there were bigger changes afoot underneath them for the updated 997. More efficient direct injection engines marked the first nod to more environmentally caring 911s, while the PDK twin-clutch gearbox arrived. Immediate response wasn’t rapturous, but after Porsche fitted the double-clutcher with proper paddles, its appeal over the clunky old Tiptronic automatic was clear. For 2009, both 3.6-liter and 3.8-liter engines receive direct injection; new PDK dual-clutch seven-speed transmission replaced the Tiptronic S automatic.

2012: Porsche debuts the all-new 991-generation 911 Carrera and Carrera S. Engine size in the base 911 once again shrinks to 3.4 liters, but horsepower increases by 5 to 350. The Carrera S keeps its 3.8-liter six, now making an even 400 horsepower and 325 lb-ft of torque.
